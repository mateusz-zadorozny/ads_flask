P-value: This number helps us understand if the difference we see between two groups is real or just happened by luck. Imagine you flip a coin 100 times and it lands on heads 60 times. The P-value helps you figure out if the coin might be special (like having a higher chance of landing on heads) or if it's just a normal coin and you got heads 60 times by chance.
For example:

  • A small P-value (less than 0.05) means the difference is probably real and not just by chance. This is usually good.
  • A large P-value (greater than 0.05) means the difference might just be due to luck.
In our case, a P-value of {{ recommendation['p_value'] }} means that there's a {{ recommendation['p_value'] * 100 }}% chance that the difference in performance between the groups is due to luck.
